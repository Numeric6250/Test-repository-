The Architecture of a Strong Answer
Central question: Why was the onset of modern economic growth in China so slow?
1
A. High-Level Framing
Reject simplistic narratives. China was not "backward" — it had markets, commerce, and technology. The issue is why it did not transition into modern economic growth.
2
B. Material Trap
Labour abundance and relative factor prices meant capital-intensive transformation was not privately profitable. The market was rational — and still failed to generate MEG.
3
C. Political-Fiscal Trap
The strongest answers weight this most heavily. Qing and Republican China lacked the fiscal and administrative capacity to finance railways, schooling, or industrial coordination.
4
D. Trade Is Not a Magic Answer
Without sufficient state capacity, external opening can deepen vulnerability. Trade and foreign technology must be understood within political-economy constraints.
The Central Claim
China's private market was not simply failing because people were irrational or anti-development. It was reproducing a stable equilibrium that was rational at the micro level, but insufficient for modern economic growth at the macro-developmental level.
Weak Answer
Taiping Rebellion · Opium Wars · Zheng He · treaty ports · Qing weakness — listed as disconnected facts
Strong Answer
Weak fiscal capacity + elite resistance limited developmental coordination → reform attempts remained partial → historical episodes illustrate this architecture
